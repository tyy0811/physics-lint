  complete-v1.0 Task 0 Step 3; secondary-source corroboration via Wikipedia
  "One-parameter group" and "Lie group–Lie algebra correspondence" articles
  and nLab "continuous homomorphisms of Lie groups are smooth").
- **Identity-component generation**: Varadarajan, V.S. (1984). *Lie Groups,
  Lie Algebras, and Their Representations.* Springer GTM 102. ISBN
  978-0-387-90969-1. §2.9–2.10 (section-level; ⚠ per
  `../_harness/TEXTBOOK_AVAILABILITY.md`). Supplies the connected-component
  structure of O(2) — identity component SO(2) plus the reflection coset
  σ · SO(2) — relevant here for Z_2 embedding.
- **ML-paper structural bridge**: Cohen, T.S. & Welling, M. (2016). *Group
  Equivariant Convolutional Networks.* ICML 2016. arXiv:1602.07576. §3–§4
  establish the G-equivariant convolution framework for discrete subgroups
  of O(2); the p4m group (C₄ rotations combined with reflections) is their
  paradigm example that realizes the Z_2 reflection subgroup of interest
  here.
- **Structural-equivalence proof-sketch** (Z_2 discrete reflection on a 2D
  periodic square grid, section-level framing throughout — no tight
  theorem-number claims per §6.4 of complete-v1.0 plan):
  1. Z_2 = {I, σ}, where σ = diag(1, −1) (axis-0 reflection) or
     σ' = diag(−1, 1) (axis-1 reflection), is a finite cyclic subgroup of
     O(2). Unlike C₄ which sits inside the identity component SO(2),
     reflections lie in the non-identity component σ · SO(2) of O(2). The
     orthogonal-group structure relevant here is covered by Varadarajan
     §2.9–2.10 (section-level): O(2) is a disconnected Lie group with two
     components (identity component SO(2) generated by any neighborhood of
     the identity, plus the reflection coset σ · SO(2)), and the Z_2
     subgroup generated by a single involution σ is a closed discrete
     subgroup whose cosets in O(2) partition it into SO(2) and σ · SO(2).
  2. Hall §2.5 (section-level, theorem number pending local copy) provides
     the one-parameter-subgroup structure of SO(2) (the identity component
     of O(2)); reflections are not themselves in any one-parameter
     subgroup of O(2), but the discrete equivariance condition
     `f(σ · u) = σ · f(u)` is well-defined independent of the
     one-parameter-subgroup machinery, since σ is a single involutive
     element with `σ² = I`.
  3. For a continuous operator f: L²(T²) → L²(T²) on the 2-torus,
     reflection-equivariance `f(σ · u) = σ · f(u)` is a well-defined
     condition on L²(T²). Hall §3.7 (section-level) — continuous
     homomorphisms of finite-dimensional matrix Lie groups are smooth —
     ensures that continuity of f plus equivariance with respect to the
     ambient O(2) action has a well-defined smooth-equivariance
     interpretation, which the discrete Z_2 restriction inherits.
  4. The rule's emitted quantity `reflect_test(f, u, axis) = ||f(σ · u) −
     σ · f(u)||_{L²} / ||f(u)||_{L²}` (defined in
     `../_harness/symmetry.py`) numerically measures deviation from this
     Z_2-equivariance condition. For operators provably reflection-
     equivariant by construction (e.g., `fft_laplace_inverse`, whose
     Fourier symbol 1/(k_x² + k_y²) is symmetric under
     (k_x, k_y) → (−k_x, k_y) and (k_x, k_y) → (k_x, −k_y) on a periodic
     square grid), the target is zero up to floating-point noise; for
     random-weight non-equivariant operators, the error lands at O(1)
     relative scale (empirically validated in
     `../_harness/tests/test_symmetry.py`).

### Correctness-fixture (CI-runnable, non-credibility-claiming)

- **Positive fixture**: `u(x,y) = cos(2πx) cos(2πy)` on
  `np.linspace(0,1,64)` — reflection-invariant on both axes:
  `np.flip(u, axis=0) ≈ u` and `np.flip(u, axis=1) ≈ u` to float noise.
  Grid 64 × 64.
- **Negative fixture**: `u(x,y) = sin(2πx) sin(2πy)` on the same grid —
  `np.flip(u, axis=k) = −u` exactly for each `k ∈ {0, 1}`, producing
  relative-L² equivariance error of precisely 2.0 per axis.
- **Operator-level correctness**: `fft_laplace_inverse` (in
  `../_harness/symmetry.py`) is proven reflection-equivariant to float
  noise in `../_harness/tests/test_symmetry.py`. The symbol
  1/(k_x² + k_y²) is symmetric under (k_x, k_y) → (−k_x, k_y) and
  (k_x, k_y) → (k_x, −k_y); zero-mode convention `û(k=0) = 0` closes the
  `(−Δ)⁻¹` kernel ambiguity. Random-weight `_NonEquivariantCNN` (learned
  positional embedding) is proven non-equivariant on the same input.
- **Rule anchor**: `test_anchor.py` directly constructs the two fixtures
  on a 64 × 64 grid, runs flip-table sanity tests, and verifies
  `ph_sym_002.check(GridField, DomainSpec(symmetries={"declared":
  ["reflection_x", "reflection_y"]}))` returns PASS on the positive
  fixture (equivariance error < 1e-12) and WARN/FAIL on the negative
  fixture (equivariance error exactly 2.0 per axis).

### Supplementary calibration context

- **Equivariance-scale calibration**: Helwig, T., Zhang, X., Fu, Y.,
  Kurtin, J.S., Wojtowytsch, S., Ji, S. (2023). *Group Equivariant
  Fourier Neural Operators for Partial Differential Equations.* ICML
  2023, PMLR 202:12907–12930. arXiv:2306.05697. Table 1 (G-FNO-p4m on

  `reflect_test`-style equivariance error on an author-constructed
  analytical fixture, and the 2D Navier–Stokes PDE family is outside
  v1.0's Laplace/Poisson/heat/wave scope. The rule's detector classifies
  a reflection-invariant field as PASS and a reflection-breaking field
  as WARN/FAIL; Helwig Table 1 supplies the scale on which that
  classification is meaningful.

each of two independent axes) only. The D_n dihedral case combining
rotation + reflection is deferred to v1.2 per complete-v1.0 plan §6.2
